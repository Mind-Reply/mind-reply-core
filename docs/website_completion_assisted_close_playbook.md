# Website Completion Assisted Close Playbook

This is an internal owner-side playbook for selling the Website Completion Package now. It is not public website copy and must not be described as active staff, guaranteed income, hidden teams, or automatic revenue.

## Goal

Convert real buyer friction into one paid package:

- Offer: Website Completion Package
- Price: GBP 600
- Buyer result: overloaded website, reply, or follow-up pressure becomes a ranked action queue, send-ready copy, and a privacy-safe receipt
- Route: MRagent first, assisted close second, invoice or payment link third

## What Makes This Sellable

MindReply should not ask people to buy technology. It should ask them to buy relief from the last mile of business communication:

- the page is nearly there but does not close;
- the offer is good but the buyer path is unclear;
- the founder or operator is overloaded and cannot decide what to fix first;
- the follow-up message needs warmth, authority, and a clean next action;
- the buyer needs one practical package rather than an open-ended agency engagement.

## Buyer Qualification

Use the package when at least two of these are true:

- They already have a website, page, offer, launch note, pitch, or client-facing message.
- The problem is broader than one reply.
- They are losing time to edits, follow-ups, hesitation, or unclear next steps.
- They need something usable inside 24 to 72 hours.
- They can pay GBP 600 without turning the sale into a long procurement process.
- They are willing to share redacted context and approve a privacy-safe receipt.

Do not sell the package when the buyer needs full brand strategy, custom engineering, legal advice, medical advice, regulated financial advice, or unredacted sensitive-data handling.

## Assisted Close Flow

1. Ask MRagent first.
   - If one message solves it, do not force the package.
   - If the pressure is the whole website, offer, follow-up route, or buyer journey, move to the package.

2. Collect only redacted context.
   - Public URL or redacted page text.
   - What is not converting or not clear.
   - The buyer's intended action.
   - Deadline.
   - Email for receipt and invoice.
   - Consent to review the redacted context.

3. Send the package frame.
   - "This fits the Website Completion Package. It is GBP 600. You get a ranked action queue, send-ready copy, and a privacy-safe delivery receipt. No long setup."

4. Ask for payment route.
   - If a payment link exists, send it.
   - If no payment link exists, ask for invoice details and send invoice instructions through the approved owner route.
   - If provider delivery is blocked, use info@mind-reply.com as the public fallback only.

5. Produce the receipt.
   - actionKind: website-completion
   - riskLevel: low-to-medium
   - confidence: medium until scope and payment route are accepted
   - rawContentRedacted: true
   - inputHash: present when generated
   - ownerDecisionNeeded: confirm scope, route invoice/payment, approve next close-ready move

## Response Templates

Positive reply:

"Good. Send the public URL or redacted text, what you want the visitor to do, and the deadline. Please do not send passwords, private addresses, API keys, or sensitive records. I will confirm whether it fits the GBP 600 package before any payment route."

Price hesitation:

"That is fair. The package is fixed at GBP 600 because it is meant to replace a messy open-ended review: ranked fixes, copy you can use, and a receipt showing what was handled. If the issue is only one reply, MRagent may be enough."

Scope expansion:

"That sounds larger than the package. The GBP 600 pass covers the buyer path, ranked action queue, send-ready copy, and receipt. Full redesign, engineering, ads, or regulated advice would need a separate scope."

Security-sensitive buyer:

"Please send only redacted context. The package can work from public pages, summaries, and removed identifiers. Raw private material should not be pasted into public channels."

Invoice route:

"I can route this as a Website Completion Package invoice request. Please send billing name, billing email, and any required reference. The delivery receipt will stay redacted."

## Daily Owner Check

Every selling day, record:

- sends made;
- replies received;
- package requests opened;
- invoice/payment links sent;
- paid confirmations;
- top objection;
- next copy or page fix that would reduce that objection.

No income is reported until payment, invoice, Stripe/Vercel Payments, or owner-confirmed sales data proves it.

## Hard Boundaries

- Do not expose personal Gmail publicly.
- Do not claim fake active teams, staff, revenue, subscribers, or integrations.
- Do not promise guaranteed growth or virality.
- Do not paste raw private pressure text into public docs.
- Do not position this as generic AI productivity software.
- Do not keep building unrelated capabilities while the payment/invoice route is unresolved.

## Next Highest-Leverage Owner Decision

Choose one route for the first paid close:

- payment link first; or
- invoice request first; or
- manual owner approval first for the first three buyers.

Until that decision is made, the website can create interest, but the close remains slower than it needs to be.
